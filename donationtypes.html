Blood Donation Types


Whole Blood

The most popular and conventional procedure is whole blood donation. A pint of blood, containing red and white cells, platelets, and plasma, is drawn. It is mostly utilized in trauma and surgery. The shelf life is 21-35 days, and donations can be made every 56 days. This is the quickest and easiest way to donate.

Requirements
  • At least 110 lbs
  • Must be 16 (with signed parent consent form) or 17+ years old
  • Always eat and drink before giving
  • Donation process takes 45 mins

Platelets

Platelets are minute blood cell fragments that help to halt or prevent bleeding. It's created from human bone marrow and has a 5-day shelf life! They are used in operations, transplants, and to assist cancer patients cope with chemo. Only at our locations can you give once every seven days.

Requirements
  • At least 110 lbs
  • Must be 17+ years old
  • Hemoglobin must be at 13.3
  • Donation process takes 1.5 hours

Plasma

Plasma is the liquid component of your blood that delivers water and nutrients to the tissues of your body. Donations can last up to a year and are used to assist burn victims and others suffering from bleeding diseases. Only at our locations can you give every 28 days.

Requirements
  • Females must be tested for HLA if they have ever been pregnant
  • Must be 17+ years old
  • Donation process takes 1.5 hours

Automated Red Cells

When just red cells are harvested, no other blood components are obtained. This is known as automated red cell donation. The donation is valid for 42 days. Red blood cells are employed in the treatment of trauma, surgery, anemia, blood loss, and diseases. Every 112 days, you can give at centers and pick mobiles.

Requirements
  • Must be 17+ years old
  • Hemoglobin must be at 13.3
  • Donation process takes 1.5 hours
